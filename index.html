Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about borehole drilling and water projects.

A typical borehole project takes 5–14 days from mobilisation to commissioning. This includes 1–2 days for geophysical survey, 2–4 days for drilling and casing, and 1–3 days for pump installation and testing. Larger multi-borehole projects may take 3–8 weeks depending on site access and geology.
Costs vary significantly based on depth, geology, pump type, and location. A basic borehole with hand pump ranges from UGX 12–25 million. A full motorised system with solar pump and storage tank typically runs UGX 35–80 million. We provide free, no-obligation site quotes after an initial consultation — contact us to start the process.
We strongly recommend it. A proper survey dramatically reduces the risk of drilling a dry hole or hitting poor-yield aquifers — which is expensive and wastes time. In areas with complex geology, a survey can be the difference between success and failure. We use resistivity surveys (VES/WENNER methods) as standard practice.
Yes. All our installations come with a 12-month warranty covering pump, pipework, and electrical components. We also offer annual maintenance contracts for pump servicing, water quality testing, and borehole camera inspections. Call or WhatsApp our support line for immediate assistance.
In Uganda, you need a borehole drilling permit from the Directorate of Water Resources Management (DWRM) and in some cases an EIA certificate from NEMA. Our team handles the entire permit application process on your behalf as part of our project package.
Yes. We offer borehole rejuvenation services including camera inspection, airlifting, surging, and chemical treatment. In many cases, a failed borehole can be restored to full yield at a fraction of the cost of redrilling. Contact us with your borehole logs and we can advise remotely.
